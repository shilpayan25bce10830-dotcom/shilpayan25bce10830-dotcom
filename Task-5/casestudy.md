Case Study: UPI & Digital Payment Scams
=======================================

Understanding UPI Fraud
-----------------------

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime in which fraudsters manipulate victims into transferring funds or disclosing their UPI PIN through carefully crafted deception tactics.

How the Scam Unfolds (Step by Step)
-----------------------------------

1.  The fraudster reaches out to the victim via phone call or WhatsApp, pretending to be a bank representative or customer care executive.
    
2.  They fabricate a story — either warning about a problem with the victim's account or dangling an offer like cashback or a reward.
    
3.  A "collect request" is then sent through UPI platforms such as GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm — this is designed to pull money _out_ of the victim's account, not deposit into it.
    
4.  The fraudster instructs the victim to enter their UPI PIN to supposedly "verify" their identity or "accept" the incoming payment.
    
5.  The moment the PIN is entered, the amount is instantly debited — with no warning and no delay.
    

Common Targets
--------------

*   Young adults and students who are newly introduced to digital payments
    
*   Elderly individuals who may be less familiar with UPI mechanics
    
*   Small shop owners and self-employed entrepreneurs
    
*   People actively searching for jobs or financial assistance online
    

Impact on Victims
-----------------

*   Immediate and often significant financial loss
    
*   Reversal of UPI transactions is extremely difficult or impossible
    
*   Emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital systems
    
*   Potential identity theft if sensitive banking details were shared
    
*   In severe cases, victims may lose their entire life savings
